# 2) Shared gameplay emotion language

Use this in all prompts to keep emotions readable across the cast.

## Core readable gameplay states

These are the **main gameplay-readable states** the portraits should communicate:

### Open / helpful

* warmer amber or saffron light
* more visible eyes
* slightly softer mouth
* shoulders more open
* less shadow across the face
* hands more relaxed or visible

### Guarded / evasive

* cooler indigo / teal shadows
* side glance or partial eye contact
* tighter mouth
* chin slightly tucked or turned away
* shoulders angled defensively
* stronger shadow cutting across one side of the face

### Stressed / flustered

* ember or coral edge highlights
* widened eyes or brow compression
* messier hair / scarf / collar tension
* more visible brushstroke turbulence
* slight facial asymmetry pushed harder
* posture tighter, more compressed, more unstable

### Annoyed / defensive

* narrowed eyes
* jaw tension
* sharper cheek and brow shadows
* mouth set or slightly skewed
* posture forward or resistant
* lighting more contrasty and confrontational

### Procedural / closed-off

* minimal emotional display
* flat mouth
* direct but controlled eye line
* squared shoulders
* cool lighting
* little warmth except perhaps one small amber edge

### Quietly concerned

* tension around eyes
* softer mouth but furrowed brow
* slight forward lean
* low warm light on one side
* face reads as burdened rather than angry
