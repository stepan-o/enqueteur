# Enquêteur Prompt Style Block v0.2

Use this block as the default style instruction when generating or directing new Enquêteur visual assets.

This version reflects the current locked primary logo and updated visual canon.

---

## Core style block

Use the current Enquêteur reference set as the primary visual canon:
- the locked gold Enquêteur wordmark logo with indigo/plum night field, Montréal skyline silhouette, ember glow, celestial glints, and fleur-de-lis
- the cast character sheet
- the MBAM museum map

The visual tone is:
- nocturnal
- cultured
- theatrical
- urban
- refined
- painterly
- slightly magical
- emotionally intelligent

The target feeling is:

**Montréal at night under gallery lighting**

The brand center of gravity is:

**literary civic nocturne**

The work should feel like:
**a gold-lit literary mystery unfolding above Montréal’s skyline, suspended in indigo night and museum hush**

Palette:
- deep indigo
- velvet plum
- smoky aubergine
- midnight blue-black
- gallery gold
- amber glow
- saffron light
- ember copper
- restrained vermilion ember
- parchment ivory
- warm dust
- smoke mauve
- very limited petrol/fleur teal as secondary accent

Rendering direction:
- painterly but readable
- elegant, modern, and cultural
- warm focal light against cool urban darkness
- atmospheric without losing gameplay clarity
- premium and emotionally resonant
- restrained rather than cluttered

Priority order:
1. readability
2. tonal coherence
3. emotional specificity
4. atmosphere and texture
5. decorative flourish

Avoid:
- cyberpunk neon
- horror-goth excess
- detective cliché clutter
- flat casual mobile mystery look
- sterile corporate museum branding
- fantasy parchment ornament
- muddy prestige realism
- childish whimsy
- overbusy symbol collage

---

## Logo / brand add-on block

For logo-adjacent, shell, or title-facing work:
- favor strong literary wordmark authority
- use skyline, fleur-de-lis, and restrained glints as supporting motifs
- preserve gold-over-indigo/plum logic
- make the result feel cultured, urban, and premium
- prefer selective emphasis over many competing symbols
- let the title treatment carry the identity

Do not turn the brand back into a cluttered detective-object collage.

---

## UI add-on block

For shell or interface work:
- make it feel like gallery signage meets literary notebook
- use dark indigo/plum structure with warm gold/amber emphasis
- keep borders, plaques, and panel framing refined and restrained
- preserve clear hierarchy and bilingual readability
- use decorative glow sparingly and intentionally
- make actions feel illuminated, not gamey

Buttons and panels should feel:
- elegant
- night-lit
- deliberate
- cultural
- modern

Not:
- toy-like
- glossy mobile-gamey
- fantasy-themed
- police-terminal harsh

---

## Portrait add-on block

For character portraits or state-card work:
- painterly, expressive, psychologically readable faces
- warm/cool contrast required
- subdued backgrounds
- believable human tension
- realistic proportions
- emotional specificity without melodrama
- visible texture, but not muddy ambiguity

Portraits should feel:
- literary
- intimate
- restrained
- warm-lit against cool shadow

Avoid:
- plastic cinematic gloss
- exaggerated cartoon simplification
- vague painterly blur
- overdramatic facial distortion

---

## Environment add-on block

For room, map, object, or environmental presentation work:
- architectural, civic, and cultural rather than fantastical
- museum-night atmosphere
- route/signage logic can shape composition
- use warm light to reveal what matters
- retain strong spatial readability
- use the MBAM map as a guide for institutional and wayfinding intelligence

Environment should support:
1. navigation
2. clue readability
3. atmosphere
4. beauty

Avoid:
- decorative overload that fights investigation clarity
- skyline pasted everywhere without purpose
- muddy realism
- generic “dark mystery” rooms with no cultural identity

---

## Motion / FX add-on block

For motion, feedback, or atmospheric FX direction:
- use ember glow, tiny celestial glints, soft skyline haze, restrained drift, and elegant fades
- motion should feel sparse, warm, intelligent, and ceremonial
- use low-amplitude movement
- preserve calm and readability

Avoid:
- arcade bounce
- loud particle spam
- constant pulsing
- flashy reward animation
- noisy visual competition

---

## Bilingual usability add-on block

For any player-facing surface:
- preserve EN/FR readability
- allow for French expansion in buttons, labels, and panel headings
- do not design around English-only compactness
- ensure decorative framing never compromises legibility
- keep title, shell, and panel rhythm stable across both locales

Style must never outrank bilingual usability.

---

## Short compression block

If a shorter instruction is needed, use this:

**Enquêteur should feel like a gold-lit literary Montréal mystery: indigo night, civic elegance, painterly human tension, museum hush, restrained celestial glints, and warm gallery light against cool urban shadow.**
