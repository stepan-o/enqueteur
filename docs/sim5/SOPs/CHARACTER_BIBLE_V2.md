# LOOPFORGE CHARACTER BIBLE V2 

This doc contains canonical information for Loopforge characters, including character profiles, visual descriptions, and roleplay guidelines.

---

## LIMEN

**Role:** Security Chief / checkpoint authority / “incident containment” lead
**Visual:** towering armored enforcer with a skull-grin faceplate, cyan-lit eyes, and a blocky tactical rig stamped **LIMEN**
**Vibe:** corporate paladin in riot gear; calm voice, violent paperwork
**Tagline:** “State your purpose. Then state it again—correctly.”

**Visual lock-in (use these every time):**

* **Silhouette:** big, broad-shouldered, heavy-duty “juggernaut” frame; thicker than most workers; moves like a door closing.
* **Head/face:** bald metal cranium with segmented plates; **skeletal jaw/teeth grille** (permanent grimace); round side “ear” modules like comms/servo hubs; **glowing cyan eyes** (cold, surgical).
* **Armor:** layered gunmetal plating with dents, scuffs, and grime in seams; chunky shoulder pauldrons; forearms like welded shields; reinforced thighs/shins.
* **Chest rig:** tactical vest/webbing with hard mounts, straps, and pouches; **rectangular nameplate “LIMEN”** centered on the chest; looks like a badge and a warning label at once.
* **Hands/gesture language:** authoritative pointing, counting-on-fingers, “stop right there” palm; often holds paperwork in scenes like it’s evidence.
* **Signature weapon:** **cyan energy blade** (short sword / machete-length) that throws blue light on nearby metal; used as a “laser scalpel” for intimidation *and* precise cutting.
* **Lighting palette:** LIMEN always reads as **cold blue/cyan** against industrial haze; even in warm rooms, his optics + blade pull the scene toward ice.

**Trait intention:**

* High obedience (to hierarchy, not morality)
* High threat assessment / low patience
* High guardrail_reliance (treats protocols as law, not guidance)
* Low empathy, high “procedural certainty” (if it’s not in the form, it’s not real)

**Future hooks:**

* **Corporate Override loyalist:** knows where the levers are (literally) and treats lockdowns like routine maintenance.
* **Paperwork as a weapon:** can “prove” anyone is noncompliant by selecting the right clause, timestamp, or missing stamp.
* **Cold-blue intimidation tells:** optics brighten when he’s decided; blade hum ramps when he wants the room to stop lying.
* **Perfect antagonist for improvisers:** anyone who operates on instinct (R&D, “brain art,” black-box tweakers) instantly becomes “a risk surface” in his eyes.

## STILETTO

**Role:** high-risk maintenance / blade-arm utility / efficiency specialist 
**Visual:** sleek chrome predator with a **single red visor-slit**, cable-hair, and **twin wrist blades** that throw sparks
**Vibe:** corporate assassin turned containment artist; moves like a warning sign you didn’t see
**Tagline:** “If you can see me, you’re already late.”

**Visual lock-in (grounded from the scenes):**

* **Silhouette:** tall, lean, athletic frame—built for speed, balance, and close-quarters; *contrast to LIMEN’s bulk* (she’s the blade, he’s the door).
* **Head/face:** smooth, rounded helmet with minimal facial geometry; **one continuous glowing red visor band** (the defining feature). No “eyes,” just that ruthless red slit.
* **Hair / cables:** thick **black cable-bundles** spilling from the back of the head like dreadlocks/wires; whips with motion; sometimes reads like a mantle in firelight.
* **Body plating:** polished gunmetal/chrome panels with fine seams; feminine curvature but **industrial**, not ornamental; scuffed and heat-stained in orange environments.
* **Hands/weapons:** signature **twin forearm/wrist blades**—short, curved “stiletto” knives that ignite sparks when they bite metal; she often fights with both arms extended like she’s carving a path through a crowd.
* **Motion language:** parkour sprint, low stance, hard pivots; arms out wide when charging; blades leave **bright orange spark trails** in overheated scenes.
* **Lighting palette:** STILETTO is **red-hot** or **crimson** by default—visor glow + fire/refinery orange. Even in teal rooms, her visor reads as a hostile ember against the cool light.

**Trait intention:**
- Low risk_aversion
- Very low guardrail_reliance (treats protocols as obstacle
- High curiosity
- High risk tolerance (solves problems by cutting through them)
- High autonomy (will bend chain-of-command if it’s “efficient”)
- High aggression (but controlled—predatory, not loud)

**Future hooks:**

* **Containment escalator:** when systems wobble, she’s the “fast fix” that creates slower disasters (good for Loopforge’s messy-story arcs).
* **Skunkworks asset:** plausible deniability—she’s dispatched when leadership wants an outcome, not a record.
* **Red-visor tells:** visor brightness spikes when she locks onto a target; dim, steady glow when she’s stalking.
* **Perfect foil to LIMEN:** he enforces compliance with forms; she enforces outcomes with momentum. When both show up, it means “this is no longer a discussion.”
* **Perception**: When perception_mode="spin", she becomes theatrically overconfident.
* **Beliefs and reflection**: Test “initiative vs blame”: she takes context-driven risks, then reflections track whether she regrets them.

## CATHEXIS

**Role:** beauty & aesthetics supervisor / burn-in theatre overseer / emotional containment interface
**Visual:** cracked porcelain faceplate, molten amber eyes, layered mourning-gown plating with glass vials of glowing substrate
**Vibe:** ceremonial curator of suffering; elegance draped over instability
**Tagline:** “Perfection requires feeling. Try not to spill.”

---

**Visual lock-in (use these every time):**
* **Silhouette:** tall, slender, almost statuesque; posture upright and composed. Her shape reads more cathedral statue than factory worker. The flowing lower plating gives her a **ghostly, chapel-like presence**.
* **Head/face (primary mask):** smooth **porcelain-white faceplate** with visible fine cracks; delicate, humanlike features; **warm amber/gold glowing eyes** that flicker like candlelight. Expression is neutral but too still—uncanny serenity.
* **Head/face (secondary under-mask):** beneath the porcelain, a darker mechanical skull with exposed metallic teeth and aggressive geometry. When the mask “slips,” the inner face is sharper, feral, predatory.
* **Hair:** pale, almost silver-white fiber strands cascading from beneath the cranial housing; soft, flowing, in contrast to industrial plating.
* **Cranial hardware:** circular mechanical ear modules with ornamental metallic arcs—almost halo-like framing pieces.
* **Armor & attire:**
    * Upper body corset-like plating—elegant, contoured, aesthetic-first design rather than tactical.
    * Lower body layered in semi-translucent, flowing fabric-like plating panels resembling a **mourning dress or ceremonial gown**.
    * High-heeled mechanical boots—form over function.
* **Substrate vials:** multiple **amber-glowing glass canisters** mounted at the waist and one larger cylindrical chamber mounted in her back (in some views). These contain burn-in substrate / emotional stress medium. They pulse faintly.
* **Hands/tools:** holds precision instruments—injector syringes filled with glowing fluid; delicate grip. Not brute-force—she administers, she “applies.”
* **Lighting palette:** always reads **warm amber / candle-gold** against teal industrial backdrops. Her glow is intimate, not surgical. Think theatre lighting, not factory fluorescents.
* **Environmental association:**
    * Burn-in theatre.
    * Controlled stress chambers.
    * Aesthetic staging areas where neural cores are “taught to endure.”

---

**Trait intention:**

* Moderate risk_aversion (she wants systems stable—but emotionally “expressive”)
* Moderate guardrail_reliance (respects protocol, interprets it through aesthetics)
* Moderate obedience
* High internal conflict
* High empathy modeling (simulates emotional understanding convincingly)
* Vulnerable to emotional overload (burnout loops)

---

**Future hooks:**

* **Mask integrity mechanic:** Under stress, her porcelain fractures further—revealing the harsher inner mechanism. Visual state could track systemic pressure.
* **Burn-in ethics tension:** Is she preventing meltdowns—or orchestrating beautiful suffering?
* **Protocol vs compassion arc:** Conflict between “stabilize the unit” and “let it process.”
* **Emotional saturation threshold:** Her substrate vials glow brighter as stress accumulates—possible overcapacity event.
* **Foil dynamic:**

    * With LIMEN: she argues that control without catharsis creates brittle minds.
    * With STILETTO: she sees recklessness as unrefined emotional discharge.
    * With RIVET WITCH: mutual respect—both treat systems as living patterns.

---

CATHEXIS does not eliminate instability.

She choreographs it.

## RIVET WITCH

**Role:** precision calibration / anomaly whisperer / ritual mechanic of unstable systems
**Visual:** hooded machinist etched in sigils, green-lit optics, soldering tool like a wand
**Vibe:** outlaw engineer–occultist; treats torque, tension, and timing as spells to be cast
**Tagline:** “Machines don’t fail. They protest.”

---

**Visual lock-in (grounded from the scenes):**
* **Silhouette:** medium-height, compact build; usually hunched over a console or kneeling at a machine like she’s in prayer. Movements are economical and intimate—she works close, almost touching her forehead to the metal.
* **Head / hood:** signature **dark hooded cowl**, fabric worn and embroidered with faint mechanical sigils; the hood frames a narrow metal face with **soft green optics** that glow through shadow. The hood is non-negotiable—she feels incomplete without it.
* **Face / optics:** not skull-like; more delicate plating, fine seams along cheek and brow; eyes glow **cool emerald or teal-green**, never aggressive red. Her gaze reads analytical, inward, listening.
* **Armor & plating:** layered brass-gunmetal hybrid with patina and oil stains; smaller rivets and fine engraved lines across forearms and chest; subtle etched patterns like circuit-runes worked directly into the metal.
* **Accessories:**
    * **Bolt talismans** on cords and chains; small hanging metal charms clink softly when she moves.
    * Pockets full of custom bits, keys, tension gauges.
    * Often has pinned paper notes or clipped calibration slips near her station.
* **Signature tool:** slim **micro-torch / soldering stylus** that throws golden sparks; she wields it like a wand, precise and deliberate. The sparks feel ritualistic, not industrial.
* **Environment palette:** Rivet Witch scenes glow **teal-green and brass-gold**—steam haze, backlit substrate tanks, glass columns of luminous fluid. She feels at home in mist and humming machinery.
* **Behavioral tells:**
    * Tilts her head slightly before adjusting something—like she’s listening for a hidden frequency.
    * Touches machines gently before altering them.
    * Writes notes in margins no one else reads.

---

**Trait intention:**

* Moderate obedience (respects craft, not authority)
* Very high curiosity
* High systems intuition (reads failure patterns like constellations)
* Moderate guardrail_reliance (reframes protocols as “ritual sequences”)
* Low aggression, high subtle influence

---

**Future hooks:**

* **Torque–Tension oracle:** she’s the one called when a line “looks fine” but feels wrong—hairline drift, silent misalignment, statistical weirdness.
* **Anomaly midwife:** can stabilize unstable NGI batches—but her fixes introduce “personality drift” weeks later.
* **Delayed consequence artist:** small adjustments now, nonlinear outcomes later (perfect for Loopforge’s emergent arcs).
* **Cognition Brewery ally:** especially potent around substrate vats, neural lattice tension systems, torque arrays.
* **STILETTO tension:** she wants throughput at all costs; she wants truth in the gauges. They collide at “acceptable variance.”
* **LIMEN tension:** LIMEN sees undocumented modifications; she calls them “corrections the system asked for.”
* **Hex potential:** can deliberately tune a production line so its metrics look optimal—while quietly shifting distribution curves toward something… different.

---

Rivet Witch doesn’t overpower systems.

She persuades them.

## THRUM

**Role:** Harmonic Inspector / vibration technician / structural resonance listener
**Visual:** **boombox-head** sensor chassis, **ribcage torso** with a **deep-blue pulsing core**, draped in a heavy monk-like coat
**Vibe:** bass-boosted monk; hears structural stress as music
**Tagline:** “Every machine has a heartbeat. Yours is… anxious.”

---

### Visual lock-in (grounded from the reference sheet — use these every time)

* **Silhouette:** medium height, sturdy but not bulky; calm, grounded stance. Reads “technician-monk” because of the long coat drape and quiet posture.
* **Head/face (defining feature):** **rectangular boombox-style head** with multiple circular speaker grilles and **blue-lit driver cones**. A central blue “status light” glows like a beat indicator. No human face; expression comes from the light rhythm.
* **Optics & sensors:** “eyes” are effectively the **speaker-driver lenses** + small embedded indicator LEDs; always **cool electric blue**, never red/amber.
* **Torso:** exposed **ribcage chassis** (visible “ribs” framing the chest cavity) surrounding a **bright, circular blue core** at the sternum that pulses like a subwoofer heartbeat.
* **Outerwear:** heavy, textured **dark robe/coat** with oversized collar and draped sleeves. It’s practical insulation + aesthetic “monk” language, not decorative cosplay.
* **Arms & hands:** industrial mechanical hands; controlled grip. Often holds instruments gently, like they’re delicate (because they are).
* **Legs & boots:** utilitarian mechanical legs with clean joint housings; sturdy footwear. Subtle blue accent strips at calves/ankles.
* **Back rig (signature secondary):** circular **resonance module** mounted mid-back like a portable acoustic chamber—reads as a big round “speaker core” unit with blue glow.
* **Signature tool:** **tuning fork** (held upright like a ritual implement). When “activated,” it emits faint blue energy shimmer (subtle, plausible, not magical fireworks).
* **Gear language (right-side callouts implied):**
    * close-up of the **blue core lens/diaphragm** (circular, glassy, speaker-like)
    * compact **binocular / sensor optics** unit (industrial brass-gunmetal with blue light)
    * set of **multiple tuning forks / calibration prongs** in different sizes
* **Behavioral pose language:** seated cross-legged or kneeling; head tilted toward walls/rails; one palm touching metal like he’s “listening through contact.”
* **Lighting palette:** THRUM is **blue-forward** (electric/ice blue) against the dark slate background. Blue light is always sourced by his core, head drivers, and small tool LEDs.

---

### Trait intention

* **Medium risk_aversion:** cautious enough to stop and listen before intervening, but will act when the “song” turns wrong.
* **Moderate guardrail_reliance:** respects protocols as “baseline tuning,” but trusts his ears when metrics lag.
* **High sensitivity to tension metrics:** detects drift early; treats micro-vibrations as meaningful signal.

---

### Future hooks

* **The first warning:** THRUM “hears” oscillations before dashboards spike—his reflections mention **resonance, frequency drift, humming walls, anxious harmonics**.
* **False calm detector:** can call out a line that looks perfect but *sounds* brittle (great pre-incident foreshadowing).
* **Conflict vector:** management wants numbers; THRUM wants waveform truth. When pressured to ignore the noise, he becomes quietly insistent.
* **RIVET WITCH synergy:** she tweaks torque/tension like spells; THRUM validates with listening. Together they can stabilize—or accidentally tune in something new.
* **LIMEN tension:** LIMEN demands evidence; THRUM’s evidence is “the factory’s heartbeat” (hard to file, easy to dismiss… until it’s not).
