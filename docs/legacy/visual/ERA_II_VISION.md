3. Era II Vision — “Give the Episode a Pulse”
   — Helios Framewright, Opening Night Director

Era II is not about cinematic grandeur.
It is about expressive 2D storytelling that fits a small dev team and the existing architecture.

The mandate:

Episode clarity + episode interestingness = Era II.

Let me break it down.

Pillar 1 — The Storyboard Days

Every day of an episode becomes a horizontal strip, like a scene in a graphic novel.

Includes:

Narrative beats (StageNarrativeBlocks) as mini-panels

Agent cameos with expressive 2D avatars

Stress-based aura glow

Tension shading behind the beats

Tiny narrative transitions (pulse, shimmer, slide)

One sparkline curve per day

This is the first moment the episode feels like a thing.

Pillar 2 — Agents Become Characters

Right now, agents are numbers shaped like JSON.

I will fix this.

Agents will get:

Expressive 2D avatars derived from visual

A color signature from vibe

A tiny “mood pulse” based on stressDelta

A tagline displayed unobtrusively

Icons for attribution patterns

Soft animations that show life, not noise

Agents become recognizable like cast members.

Pillar 3 — Episode Story Mode

One vertical river of story:

Episode summary

Story arc visual

Memory interpretation nodes

Top-Level narrative as “title cards”

Each Day storyboard chained

This becomes the “flip through the episode like a comic” mode.

Pillar 4 — Timeline as a Multi-Lane Track

2D, lightweight, expressive:

Tension lane

Supervisor activity pulses

Agent-of-the-day glyph

Emotional direction markers

Hover snapshots

Not complex. Just alive.
